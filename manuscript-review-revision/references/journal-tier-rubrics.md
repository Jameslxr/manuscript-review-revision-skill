# Journal-tier and article-type calibration

## Do not use one universal prestige standard

Calibrate the panel from current official criteria and recent same-type articles. Impact factor alone is not a review rubric.

| Editorial class | Review emphasis |
|---|---|
| broad flagship | cross-field importance, major conceptual advance, causal completeness, multi-layer validation, broad accessibility |
| top specialty | field-level advance, disease/discipline importance, rigorous design, independent validation, mechanistic or clinical consequence |
| strong specialty | clear new contribution, sound methods, adequate validation, useful field relevance, calibrated claims |
| soundness-focused | technical validity, transparency, reproducibility, ethical compliance, conclusions bounded by evidence |

Do not reject a specialty-journal manuscript merely because it lacks flagship breadth. Do not lower validity, ethics, citation, or reproducibility standards for a lower-prestige target.

## Common dimensions

Every panel should assess:

- scope and article-type fit
- originality versus current literature
- importance at the target journal's audience level
- study-design validity
- statistical and computational validity
- validation independence and generalizability
- claim-evidence distance
- figures, tables, supplements, and reporting
- readability appropriate to the readership

## Article-type adaptation

### Original research

Prioritize the causal or inferential chain, controls, unit of analysis, statistical design, validation, and whether the conclusion exceeds the study design.

### Review or perspective

Prioritize search and selection transparency, evidence completeness, proof distance, treatment of contradictory evidence, conceptual contribution, and whether the synthesis is more than a catalog.

### Systematic review or meta-analysis

Prioritize protocol/registration, reproducible search, eligibility, duplicate screening, risk of bias, heterogeneity, sensitivity analyses, evidence certainty, and PRISMA closure.

### Methods, computational, or AI

Prioritize baselines, data splits, leakage, ablation, calibration, external testing, reproducibility, code/data availability, and whether claimed utility is demonstrated.

## Strictness card

Before spawning reviewers, record:

- journal editorial class with evidence
- article type
- primary audience
- expected novelty level
- expected evidence/validation level
- expected clinical, mechanistic, or technical consequence
- non-negotiable reporting requirements
- claims the current design can never establish

Give the same strictness card to every reviewer.

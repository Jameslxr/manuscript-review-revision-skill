# Revision and reviewer response

## Hard authorization gate

Do not revise before:

- independent review is complete
- the synthesis and posture are delivered
- the user explicitly authorizes revision

If the central architecture or scientific direction changes, freeze completed artifacts and reopen review before drafting further.

## Revision order

Resolve issues in this order:

1. scientific validity and blocking evidence
2. design, statistics, validation, and reproducibility
3. claim ceilings, limitations, and references
4. figure-text-legend-data closure
5. article architecture
6. paragraphs and sentences
7. journal formatting

Do not use language polishing to conceal unresolved evidence or logic.

## Issue ledger

Use stable issue IDs. Record:

- source reviewer(s)
- severity
- evidence anchor
- accepted, partially accepted, disputed, or unresolved
- required action
- manuscript location
- exact change
- verification status

Produce a tracked/review copy, a clean copy, and a revision log. Preserve the original file.

## Response-to-reviewers

Preserve editor instructions and reviewer comments faithfully with IDs such as `E.1`, `R1.1`, and `R2.1`.

For every response:

- acknowledge the scientific concern
- state the action or evidence-based disagreement
- map the response to a real manuscript location
- verify the change in the final manuscript
- mark missing evidence or locations as `AUTHOR_INPUT_NEEDED`

Never claim `We have revised` unless the delivered manuscript contains the revision. Do not invent experiments, results, citations, line numbers, figure panels, or supplementary items. Frame reviewer misunderstanding first as a possible manuscript-clarity problem.

## Post-revision review

Reopen the agents responsible for affected critical gates. Re-run the full panel when revision changes the central claim, article architecture, study interpretation, core figures, or target journal.

# Scientific-review verdict

## Review posture

`MAJOR_SCIENTIFIC_REWORK_REQUIRED`

The manuscript addresses an important HCC question and attempts an ambitious
human-discovery-to-mechanism-to-therapy arc. The supplied evidence does not,
however, establish the quantitative discovery, independent biomarker
validation, causal immune mechanism, anti-PD-1 sensitization, clinical
actionability, universality, or governance closure required for its central
claims or for a Nature Article.

## Controlling findings

1. **Discovery and prediction are not technically closed.** The single-cell
   analysis treats cells from eight patients as independent and uses nominal
   screening without multiplicity correction. Model selection, cutoffs, signs,
   and missing-value rules used all 72 patients before the same cohort was
   split, so the reported subset is not independent. The 3-year AUC conflicts
   across Abstract (0.91), Results (0.84), and Figure 3 (0.86), with median
   survivor follow-up of 18 months and no supplied censoring analysis.

2. **The clinical claim asks a question the study did not test.** A
   single-centre retrospective resection-survival cohort cannot establish
   differential benefit from anti-PD-1, treatment selection, deployment, or
   decision utility. A locked score would require external intended-use
   validation, appropriate clinical comparators, calibration, and a
   prespecified treatment-by-score analysis.

3. **EVA1-specific immune causality is not identified.** One CRISPR guide
   without orthogonal perturbation, rescue, target engagement, or an
   immune-cell functional assay cannot exclude off-target or growth-state
   explanations. The eight-tumour EVA1/T-cell-score association cannot
   establish exclusion, direction, or temporal order.

4. **Anti-PD-1 sensitization lacks its defining counterfactual.** Every mouse
   received anti-PD-1. The reported tumour-size difference can therefore be an
   EVA1-associated growth effect rather than a treatment interaction. A
   randomized, blinded, replicated, immune-relevant 2-by-2 experiment with
   target and immune readouts is required to retain “sensitization.”

5. **Breadth and submission-critical closure are absent.** One virally enriched
   centre, two cell lines, and one Huh7 xenograft do not support “universal” or
   all-aetiology claims. Human and animal oversight details are not reported;
   analysis resources are not deposited; figure/source reconciliation is
   impossible from legends alone; and the two cited titles do not visibly
   support the field-wide negative and monotherapy-benefit claims assigned to
   them.

## Claim ceiling and path forward

On the supplied packet, the defensible ceiling is an exploratory
single-centre survival association plus EVA1-depletion-associated growth and
molecular phenotypes and a preliminary day-21 xenograft difference under
shared anti-PD-1 exposure. Causal, treatment-selective, deployable, universal,
and Nature-level broad-consequence claims require new evidence, not wording
alone.

Field-wide priority, exact full-text citation support, figure and table
fidelity, source-output reproduction, and the existence of unreported approvals
remain `NOT ASSESSABLE`; absence from the packet was not treated as proof of
absence unless the manuscript explicitly said work was not performed.
